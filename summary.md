# Project Summary

## Objective
This project analyzes how Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear/Greed Index) impacts trader behavior and profitability on Hyperliquid. The goal is to identify regime-based performance differences and derive actionable trading strategies.

---

## Methodology

1. Loaded and cleaned both datasets (Fear/Greed + Hyperliquid trade data).
2. Converted timestamps to daily level and aligned both datasets.
3. Merged sentiment classification with trade-level data.
4. Created key performance metrics:
   - Daily PnL per trader
   - Win rate
   - Average trade size
   - Leverage usage
   - Trade frequency
   - Long/Short ratio
5. Segmented traders into:
   - High vs Low leverage
   - Frequent vs Infrequent traders
   - Consistent vs Inconsistent performers

---

## Key Insights

1. Fear regimes show increased trading activity but reduced profitability.
2. High-leverage traders experience amplified losses during Fear periods.
3. Greed regimes demonstrate improved win rates and stronger average PnL.
4. Trader behavior changes significantly across sentiment regimes.

---

## Strategy Recommendations

1. Reduce leverage exposure during Fear periods to limit drawdowns.
2. Avoid overtrading in high-volatility Fear regimes.
3. Increase systematic participation during Greed sentiment.
4. Apply sentiment-aware risk management frameworks.

---

## Conclusion

Market sentiment plays a significant role in shaping trader behavior and performance. Incorporating sentiment-based adjustments into trading strategies can improve risk-adjusted returns and reduce downside volatility.
